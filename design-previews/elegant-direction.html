i.

The proposition

A ballroom syllabus is more like an opera program than a SaaS dashboard. It deserves paper, ink, hairline rules, and a quiet typographic voice — interrupted, just occasionally, by the gleam of metal. This direction is restraint everywhere except where rank and achievement live; there, the cards turn into actual bronze, silver, and gold.

Choose monochrome paper. Choose ink. Choose hairlines. Then put real metal in the places where a dancer earns their place. — The brief, in one breath
ii.

The palette

Two neutrals, one signature, three metals. The metals are not part of the page — they are events that appear on the page.

iii.

The type

Playfair Display is the voice of the building — italic for emphasis, small caps for labels, drop caps for openings. Noto Sans is the quiet machinery. EB Garamond appears only in long-form prose: figure descriptions, syllabus notes, editorial copy.

iv.

The metals

Five treatments are proposed. They share a four-stop pigment stack (shadow · base · highlight · gleam) and differ only in how light meets the surface: a straight pour, a brushed spin, a darkened patina, an inlay into type itself, or a near-invisible whisper for dense lists. All built from CSS gradients — no canvas.
