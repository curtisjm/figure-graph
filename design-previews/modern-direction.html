i / proposition

A quieter page, a louder medal.

Same metal system as Direction No. 1. Different framing around it. Where The Program uses warm parchment, italic emphasis, and Roman numerals, The Atelier uses cool white, monospace identifiers, and architectural grids. The page becomes a workshop instead of a playbill — and the metals stand out even more because the surfaces have nothing to compete with them.

ii / palette

No chromatic signature.

Black, white, three tiers of graphite. The metals are the only color in the entire interface — so when bronze, silver, or gold appears, it means something.

Material names, not UI signals — wine, sage, clay. All desaturated, all hovering around the same lightness, so they feel like a set. Apply only to genuinely semantic states (a withdrawn registration, a saved confirmation, a payment that hasn't settled) — never as decoration.

iii / type

A variable serif. A neutral sans. Monospace identifiers.

Fraunces is the variable-font sister of Playfair — same editorial bones, more contemporary calibration, far more controllable across sizes. Inter for body and UI. JetBrains Mono for figure numbers, beat counts, file IDs, dates — anything that wants to look like data.
